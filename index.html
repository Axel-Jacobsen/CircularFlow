Conformal Mapping for creating airfoils

It is easy to find aerodynamic lift around a cylinder; it is very difficult to find it (organically at least) around an airfoil. We can use something called a Joukowsky Transform to transform a cylinder to an airfoil. Since the Joukowsky Transform is a conformal mapping, we can use the same equation for lift as we can for the cylinder.
w to move the circle up
s to move the circle down
a to move the circle left
d to move the circle down
r to reset the circle

Taylor Couette flow

"No Slip" boundary conditions are boundary conditions which say that the relative velocity of the fluid next to a solid bounary is zero. For example, if a boat is moving through the water, the water molecules right beside the hull move with the boat.
